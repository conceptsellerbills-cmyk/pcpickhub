## Monitor Buying Guide

**Resolution vs size:** 27" at 4K is the sweet spot for desktop use. 27" at 1080p looks soft. 1440p works well at 27-32".

**Panel type:**
- **IPS:** Best color accuracy and viewing angles — recommended for most use
- **VA:** Best contrast ratio, good for dark room gaming
- **OLED:** Best of everything but premium price and burn-in risk with static content
- **TN:** Fast response but poor colors — mostly obsolete

**Refresh rate:** 144Hz+ for gaming. 60Hz fine for productivity. 240Hz+ for competitive gaming.

**Response time:** GtG (gray-to-gray) time affects motion clarity. 1ms GtG is marketing; look for MPRT (moving picture response time) for more meaningful comparison.

**HDR:** True HDR requires 1,000+ nits brightness and local dimming. "HDR400" on budget monitors is mostly marketing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is 4K worth it for gaming?
Yes if you have a GPU that can push 4K (RTX 4070 Ti Super minimum). Without adequate GPU power, 4K at 60fps often looks worse than 1440p at 144fps.

### Should I get a curved monitor?
For ultrawide, yes — curvature improves immersion and reduces distortion at the edges. For standard widescreen, it's personal preference.

### How important is monitor calibration?
For professional color work: essential. For gaming and general use: factory calibration on quality monitors is typically acceptable.
