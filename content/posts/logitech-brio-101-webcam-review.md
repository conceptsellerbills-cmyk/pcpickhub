## Video Quality: Full HD 1080p at 30fps

The Brio 101 shoots at **1920×1080 (Full HD) at 30fps** — the standard resolution for professional video conferencing. At this resolution, the difference versus a 720p laptop webcam is immediately visible: your face appears sharper, skin tones render more accurately, and your background is clearly defined rather than a blurry smear.

The image sensor on the Brio 101 includes **automatic light correction**, which compensates for common lighting scenarios like sitting in front of a bright window (backlit environments) or working in a dim room in the evening. It won't perform miracles in pitch black, but for typical home office and corporate environments, the auto-exposure keeps your face well-lit without manual adjustment.

**Field of view** is 70 degrees — a standard, natural framing that keeps you centered without the fish-eye distortion you see on wide-angle webcams. For solo video calls and streaming, 70° provides a clean, professional frame. If you need to fit multiple people in frame, the wider-angle Brio 500 would be a better fit, but for single-person use, 70° is ideal.

**Autofocus** is fixed-focus on the Brio 101 — meaning the lens is pre-set for the typical webcam-to-face distance (50cm–1.5m). For desk use, this is fine and eliminates the hunting/pulsing behavior that plagues cheap autofocus webcams. The trade-off is that macro shots (showing documents or products up close) won't be sharp.

## Built-In Microphone

The Brio 101 includes a **built-in omnidirectional microphone** positioned to capture your voice clearly from a typical desk setup. The microphone performs solidly for its price tier — voice is intelligible, background noise is manageable, and you won't get the echoing hollow sound of laptop mics that sit at keyboard level trying to reach your face.

<img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/71R1E5hur3L._AC_SL1500_.jpg" alt="Logitech Brio 101 privacy shutter and microphone detail" style="width:100%;max-width:700px;display:block;margin:24px auto;border-radius:12px" />

For high-stakes audio situations (podcasting, music, voice-over work), you'll want a dedicated USB microphone. But for Zoom calls, Teams meetings, and casual streaming, the Brio 101's built-in mic is fully capable and eliminates the need for a separate audio device.

The microphone picks up sound in a roughly 180-degree arc facing the camera, meaning it captures your voice whether you're speaking directly at the lens or looking slightly off-center at a second monitor.

## Privacy Shutter

One of the Brio 101's most practical features is its **built-in physical privacy shutter** — a sliding cover that physically blocks the lens when closed. This is not a software setting or a status LED; it's a mechanical barrier that makes it physically impossible for the camera to transmit video even if software is running.

In 2025, with video conferencing as a constant part of the workday, a physical shutter is a professional necessity rather than a paranoia feature. The Brio 101's shutter slides smoothly and clicks securely into both open and closed positions, so you always know its state at a glance.

## Plug-and-Play: No Software Required

The Brio 101 is **USB plug-and-play**. Connect it via USB-A, and your operating system (Windows 10/11, macOS 12+, ChromeOS) recognizes it immediately as a UVC-compliant webcam. No drivers, no software, no Logitech account — just plug in and select it as your camera in Zoom, Teams, or any other application.

For IT-managed environments and corporate setups, plug-and-play compliance is a significant advantage. There's no software installation request to clear through IT, no admin privileges required, and no background processes running after the call.

Logitech does offer optional **Logi Tune** software for Windows and macOS users who want to adjust brightness, contrast, and framing, but it's completely optional. Out of the box, the auto settings are well-calibrated for most environments.
